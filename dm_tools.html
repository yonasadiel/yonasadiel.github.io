SYSTEM_MECHANICS_PROTOCOLS

Dice & Rolls (p.16, 53)

Actions are resolved using six-sided dice (D6).

Success

Roll dice equal to relevant Attribute score. A 6 (Sentre logo on custom dice) is a success. At least one 6 needed.

Pushing Rolls

If a roll fails or more successes are desired, you can push. Re-roll any dice not showing a 1 (skull) or 6 (Sentre logo). All dice count after re-roll. For each 1 (skull) on a BASE DIE after pushing, lose 1 Hope. Cannot re-roll dice showing skull or gear damage symbols.

Gear Dice

Use dice of a different color. 6s are successes. A 1 (gear damage symbol) on a GEAR DIE when pushing reduces the gear's bonus by 1 (if bonus hits 0, gear is Busted). Does NOT reduce Hope.

Multiple Successes

More 6s = better outcome (e.g., more damage, faster/quieter action, additional positive effects as per GM).

Helping

Up to 3 others can help, giving +1 die each if they can logically assist. In combat, helping uses the helper's turn.

Opposed Rolls (p.54)

When facing active resistance (e.g., convincing a skeptical NPC, sneaking past a guard). Both sides roll. Active party needs MORE successes than opponent. Only active party can push. Tension between Travelers adds bonus dice.

Modifiers

GM or rules can grant bonus dice (e.g., +1 die) or penalty dice (e.g., -2 dice). Never roll fewer than one die.

Attributes & Core Stats (p.52, 54-55)

Attributes (p.52)

Rated 2-6. Roll 4D6 for starting values, re-roll 1s until all dice are 2+. Assign to:

  • Strength (STR): Toughness, endurance, physical force.
  • Agility (AGI): Coordination, dexterity, speed, aiming.
  • Wits (WITS): Intellect, awareness, knowledge, problem-solving.
  • Empathy (EMP): Social skills, understanding others, persuasion, deception.

Low starting total (â‰¤15) grants an extra talent.

Health (p.54)

Physical well-being. Max Health = (STR + AGI) / 2, rounded up. Reaching 0 Health = Incapacitated.

Hope (p.55)

Mental resilience, willpower. Max Hope = (WITS + EMP) / 2, rounded up. Reaching 0 Hope = Breakdown.

Bliss (p.55, 92-93)

Accumulated from using neuronics. Each failed neuro-roll = +1 Bliss. If Bliss â‰¥ Hope, user is trapped in the Electric State. Reduced by 1 per day (no neurocaster), but D6 roll of 1 makes that point of Bliss permanent.

Combat Overview (p.78-81)

Sequence

  1. Initiative (p.78): Determine attacker/defender sides. Attackers act first, then defenders.
  2. Turns (p.78): Each combatant gets 1 move + 1 action OR 2 moves. Move must precede action.

Range Bands (p.78)

  • Engaged: Melee.
  • Short: Same zone.
  • Medium: Adjacent zone.
  • Long: Up to 4 zones away.
  • Extreme: Further.

Close Combat (p.80)

STR roll. Target takes hit or fights back (opposed STR vs STR). Damage based on weapon + extra 6s.

Ranged Combat (p.80)

AGI roll. Target stands tall or dodges (opposed AGI vs AGI). Cover grants armor. Damage based on weapon + extra 6s.

Ambushes (p.81)

AGI roll to surprise. Unaware target cannot fight back/dodge. -3 dice for close combat ambush.

Damage, Healing & Conditions (p.82-89)

Taking Damage (p.82)

Reduces Health. At 0 Health = Incapacitated.

  • Death Rolls: 4D6 each turn while Incapacitated. 3 total 6s = Stabilized. 3 failed rolls (fewer than 3 6s accumulated) = Death.
  • Rallying: EMP roll by another to restore some Health.
  • Stabilizing: Medic talent + WITS roll stops death rolls.

Serious Injuries (p.84)

If Incapacitated but survive, roll on Serious Injury table (see PDF p.84 for full table). Effects vary, some need Surgery (Surgeon talent, WITS roll).

Healing (p.84)

1 Health per Shift if resting (2 with Nurse care). Blocked by hunger/disease.

Hope Loss & Breakdown (p.85)

From pushing rolls or Traumatic Events (resist with EMP). At 0 Hope = Breakdown (cannot act meaningfully).

  • Rallying: EMP roll by another to restore some Hope.
  • Hope Recovery: Reduce Tension with other Travelers, or use specific gear. Not automatic while hungry/sleep-deprived.

Mental Trauma (p.87)

If suffer Breakdown, roll on Mental Trauma table (see PDF p.87 for full table). Weekly WITS/EMP roll to recover.

Other Hazards (p.88-89)

Explosions (Blast Power), Cold (STR rolls or damage), Fire (Intensity), Disease (Virulence vs STR), Falling, Hunger/Thirst (STR rolls or damage, blocks recovery), Sleep Deprivation (WITS rolls or fall asleep, blocks Hope recovery).

Neuronics In-Depth (p.90-97)

Neurocasters (p.90, 92)

Helmets to access neuroscapes. Attributes: Processor, Network, Graphics (gear dice). Damaged by pushing rolls. Busted if attribute = 0. Wired connection = +2 dice.

Neuroscapes (p.92, 133)

Virtual worlds. Local or Global. Bliss accumulation (p.92). If Bliss â‰¥ Hope = Trapped. Describe using Type, Theme, Mood (PDF p.133).

Actions in Neuroscape

  • Finding Information (p.94): WITS roll (+Processor, +Data Miner talent). Difficulty (1-3) = # of successful rolls needed. Each roll = 1 Stretch (or 1 Shift if previous failed).
  • Hacking Systems (p.94): WITS roll (+Network, +Hacker talent) vs system Difficulty (1-3). To disable for Stretch. +1 Difficulty to control, +1 Difficulty for Shift duration. Each roll = 1 Stretch. Failure may trigger alarms.
  • Interacting with Avatars (p.97):
    • Communication/Persuasion: EMP roll (+Graphics, +Gamer talent) vs opponent's WITS (+Network).
    • Manipulation: Series of EMP rolls over time to change core beliefs.
    • Avatar Combat: Close combat rules, but use WITS. +Graphics, +Gamer. Damage reduces user's Health. 0 Health = Incapacitated, disconnect, mental trauma.

Intercerebral Intelligences (p.97)

Sentient entities native to neuroscapes. Have WITS/EMP, don't need casters.

Drones (p.98-99)

Remote-controlled robots. Require neurocaster. Use drone's STR/AGI, user's WITS/EMP. Neurocaster's Network attribute as gear dice for all rolls. Drones have Hull (like Health) and Armor. Hull 0 = drone inoperable, operator disconnects. Failed rolls controlling drone = +1 Bliss for operator.

Consumer Drones (Example from p.99)

  • Kid Kosmo: STR 3, AGI 5, Hull 8, Armor 2, Dmg 1 (Engaged)
  • Wally Wayne: STR 4, AGI 5, Hull 9, Armor 2, Dmg 1 (Short-Medium)

(See PDF p.99 for full table)

Military Drones & Drone Growths

Military drones are heavily armed/armored. Drone Growths are mysterious, often large, jury-rigged drones, possibly linked to Intercerebral Intelligences (see Threats tab).

Vehicles & Chases (p.101-107)

Vehicle Attributes (p.101)

Passengers, Maneuverability (gear dice for driving), Speed (zones/round in combat), Hull, Armor. Traits (PDF p.102) can modify. Fuel tracked abstractly.

Vehicle Movement & Stunts (p.103)

Driving in combat: Speed rating = # of moves. Stunts (jumping, ramming) are actions, require AGI roll (+Maneuverability). Failed roll = Accident (see PDF tables p.104).

Vehicle Damage (p.105)

Suffer damage to Hull. Hull 0 = Wrecked. Component damage if take â‰¥ half Hull in one hit (roll D6 on table, PDF p.105). Maneuverability reduced by pushing rolls; if 0, inoperable.

Chases (p.106-107)

Abstract range categories. Open opposed AGI roll (+Maneuverability) each round. Winner adjusts range by # of extra 6s. Range > Extreme = escape. Range Engaged = ram/attack. Obstacles (PDF p.107) can complicate.

THREAT_ANALYSIS_REPORTS

Violent Threats (p.138-140)

Primarily use force or intimidation.

Law Enforcement

Stats: STR 4, AGI 4, WITS 3, EMP 3, Health 4. Talent: Pistoleer. Gear: Handgun/shotgun, patrol car, $100.
Tactics: Uphold law (often corruptly), roadblocks, questioning, arrests, potential for excessive force.

Secret Agents

Stats: STR 4, AGI 5, WITS 3, EMP 2, Health 5. Talent: Stealthy. Gear: Handgun/assault rifle, black van, $200.
Tactics: Surveillance, deception, espionage, kidnapping, assassination. Highly loyal to mission.

Gang Members

Stats: STR 3, AGI 4, WITS 3, EMP 3, Health 4. Talent: Knifeman. Gear: Knife/bat/handgun, neurine, D6x$10, vehicle.
Tactics: Robbery, extortion, territorial violence, intimidation. Motivations vary (survival to sadism).

Crazed Killers

Stats: STR 5, AGI 5, WITS 3, EMP 4, Health 5. Talent: Knifeman. Gear: Knife, shotgun, car, $50.
Tactics: Unpredictable violence, often driven by delusion or trauma. May be manipulated by other forces.

Manipulative Threats (p.142-145)

Use influence, lies, and social engineering.

Cultists

Stats: STR 3, AGI 4, WITS 3, EMP 5, Health 4. Talent: Charmer. Gear: Holy symbol/texts, knife.
Tactics: Recruitment through charisma/promises, isolation of members, psychological manipulation, fanatical devotion to leader/ideology. Often hostile to outsiders or those rejecting their beliefs.

Local Strongmen

Stats: STR 4, AGI 3, WITS 4, EMP 5, Health 4. Talent: Menacing. Gear: Handgun, $200.
Tactics: Exploit chaos for power, offer protection for loyalty, use fear/prejudice, eliminate rivals. May seem charming initially.

Business Leaders

Stats: STR 3, AGI 3, WITS 5, EMP 4, Health 3. Talent: Con artist. Gear: Handgun, $500.
Tactics: Scams, exploitation for profit, disregard for well-being of others. Motivated by greed, often hiding behind a veneer of legitimacy.

Technological Threats (p.146-149)

Malfunctioning or malevolent AI, drones, robots.

Robots

Example Stats: STR 6, AGI 4, Hull 5, Armor 6. Weapons: Vice grip (Dmg 2), taser.
Tactics: Follow programming (which may be corrupted), guard duty, automated tasks. Can be hacked. Some rumored to gain sentience.

Drone Growths

Example Stats: STR 9, AGI 6, WITS 8, EMP 3, Hull 10, Armor 6. Weapons: Stomp (Dmg 2), Electric Blast (Dmg 2), Neuroscape Ray gun (Dmg 2).
Tactics: Physical manifestations of Intercerebral Intelligences. Often large, bizarrely constructed. Can act in real world and neuroscape. May seek to assimilate humans or resources.

Systems

Bodiless AIs in networks. Confronted via neurocasting.
Tactics: Control networks, manipulate connected devices/drones/people, information warfare, self-preservation, expansion. May have complex, alien motivations.

Environmental Threats (p.150)

Extreme Weather

Hurricanes, blizzards, heatwaves. Cause direct damage, destroy resources, block paths, create survival challenges (cold, fire, falling debris).

Disease

Epidemics or bio-weapons. Virulence rating (p.89). Spread, cause illness, death. Can create panic and social breakdown.

Other

Earthquakes, wildfires, floods, landslides, animal attacks, pollution zones.

WORLD_LORE_DATABASE

The Setting: Alternate 1997 (p.10, 20-22)

The year is 1997. The USA was fractured by a devastating Civil War (1975-1984). Society is in slow collapse, with widespread decay and infrastructure breakdown. Many seek escape in neuronics. The '90s pop culture is present but juxtaposed with advanced, often dangerous, technology and a bleak future.

Neuronics Deep Dive (p.31-37)

Core Concept

Technology mimicking human brain functions, enabling direct mind-machine interface.

Drones (p.31, 98)

Remotely piloted machines, from household units to massive war machines. Key part of the Civil War.

Robots (p.32, 98)

Automated machines, less precise than drones. Used for labor, guard duty. Some rumored to be evolving.

Neurocasters (p.32, 90)

Helmet-like devices to access networks, pilot drones, enter neuroscapes. Require neurine and Vanadium Redox batteries. Mode 6 (p.34) update (1996) made neuroscapes hyper-addictive.

Neuroscapes (p.33, 92)

Vivid virtual worlds. Can be local or global. Highly addictive, leading many to abandon reality.

Neurine (p.34, 109)

Chemical for neurocasters, also a highly addictive drug ("dream glint").

Techno Cults (p.34)

Groups worshipping machines or network entities. The Convergence (p.36) is a major, secretive cult worshipping an "Intercerebral Divinity," linked to stillbirths among drone pilots and strange phenomena.

Drone Growths & Intercerebral Intelligences (p.34, 97, 149)

Monstrous, jury-rigged drones appearing in the wastes. Possibly physical forms of AIs (Intercerebral Intelligences) born in neuroscapes.

Pacifica In Detail (p.38-44)

Formerly California. Officially a republic led by President Latoya Griffin, but heavily influenced (controlled) by Sentre Corp. Capital: Sacramento. Largest city: Los Angeles.

Key Locations:

  • Los Angeles: Rebuilt Hollywood, Sentre skyscraper HQ. Neurograph towers are ubiquitous. High contrast between wealth and decay. Rumors of secret corporate experiments.
  • Sacramento: Political/economic center. Largest neurograph tower. Rapid growth from refugees.
  • San Francisco: More freedom, fewer neuro-towers, lower addiction rates. Hub for free-thinkers, artists. Notable library system.
  • North & East (Sierra Nevada, Yosemite): Less law, some unspoiled nature. Rumors of mutated plants resisting neuro-tech in Yosemite. Shanty towns (e.g., Lake Tahoe). "Bird Heads" (anti-neurotech veterans) near Happy Camp.
  • Coast & Islands: Small communities, some with unique local neuroscapes. Morro Bay: techno-monks creating virtual Heaven. Channel Islands: rumors of old military bases, experimental weapon sites. Big Sur: rebel groups.

Problems:

Earthquakes, wildfires, pollution from Nevada desert, water shortages, diseases (e.g., Rot Eyes), social unrest, heavily armed militias.

Major Corporations (p.44)
  • Sentre: Multinational, dominates all neuro-technology. Run by Valeria Lopez (rarely seen). Their logo (eye with red S) is everywhere.
  • Happy-Cool: Neurocasters/neuroscapes for kids. Symbol: smiling yellow head. Expanding to elderly, pets.
  • LifeBuddy: "Assisted afterlife" services. Controversial contracts for elderly. Avatars of the "departed" met in neuroscapes.
  • Grizzly Burger: Major food supplier. Rumored to use actual grizzly meat in 1/100 burgers.
Other Nations (Post-USA) (p.46-47)
  • Northern Union: (WA, OR) Fragile democracy, linked to Pacifica. Rumored anti-addiction research labs angering Pacifica.
  • Blackwelt Exclusion Zone: (NV) Mostly desert, ruins from drone bombardments. Some communities thrive (Henderson, Reno, parts of Vegas). Underground market for restored luxury goods.
  • Libertania: (UT, CO, AZ, NM) Relatively stable, guards southern border. Addison Art Retreat (Santa Fe) focuses on neuro-art.
  • South Republic: (TX, LA, OK+) Sees itself as true USA remnant. High Sentre neurocaster adoption, now crumbling fast due to Mode 6 addiction.
  • Canada: Strengthened, capital Toronto. Suspicious of neuro-tech, financial problems, illegal neuro-tech smuggling.
  • Mexico: Dominated by warlords, constant clashes. Porous border with Pacifica, issues with neurine labs.

    JOURNEY_PLANNING_PROTOCOLS

    Planning the Journey (p.112-117)
    1. Starting Point & Destination: Choose based on Pacifica map (Ch.2) and Traveler Dreams.
    2. Route: Mark on map. Decide player control over route (fixed vs. flexible).
    3. Journey Length (p.114): One-shot (1 Stop), Short (2-4), Medium (4-7), Long (8-12). Affects pacing and Threat introduction.
    4. Personal Goals (p.62, 114): Specific objectives for each Traveler, tied to their Dream and often the Destination. Add a "Kicker" (inciting incident).
    5. Personal Threats (p.62, 115): Obstacles/antagonists for each Traveler, linked to their Goal. Each has a GM-designed Countdown of escalating events.
    6. Vehicle (p.62, 101): Describe type, model, traits. Starts with half tank fuel. Shared items (PDF p.63).
    Preparing Stops (p.118-127)

    A Stop is a limited area, focus of 1-3 sessions. Key elements:

    • Setting (p.118): Location, environment, population, mood. Use tables (PDF p.119-120) for terrain, population, communication, size, prosperity, weather. Add Electric State elements & '90s nostalgia (PDF p.120).
    • Blocker (p.123): What makes Travelers stop (e.g., roadblock, vehicle malfunction, urgent need). Can be hard or soft. Table on PDF p.122.
    • Needs (p.123): Traveler requirements (food, fuel, safety, info). Table on PDF p.122.
    • The Situation (p.123): Background, current conflicts at the Stop. Conflict generator table on PDF p.124.
    • Threats (p.123, Ch.6): Antagonists at the Stop. Types: Violent, Manipulative, Technological, Environmental, Past Sins, Personal.
    • Countdown (p.125): Escalating events triggered by GM to advance plot. Examples: Threat presentation, attack, lies spread, victims appear, sabotage, capture, deal offered.
    • Locations (p.126): Key places within the Stop. Map them out. Example locations table on PDF p.126.
    Playing the Stop (p.128-133)

    Starting a Stop Session

    Set scene before arrival, discuss Travelers' status, roleplay journey, describe arrival at Blocker.

    GM Responsibilities

    React to players, activate Threats/Countdown, use NPCs (rumors, help requests), point out Needs, cut scenes. Maintain pacing. (GM "What To Do?" list PDF p.128)

    Making it Come Alive (p.132)

    Use sensory details, themes, ask players questions, add twists, '90s nostalgia, Electric State elements, NPC voices, show art, maintain mystery. Emphasize "World is Falling Apart" theme.

    Information Gathering (p.133)

    Encourage NPC interaction or neurocasting. Improvise info not in prep.

    Neuroscape Scenes (p.133)

    Keep brief if solo Traveler. Describe using Type, Theme, Mood (table PDF p.133). Focus on objectives like finding info or hacking.

    Ending the Journey (p.118)

    Travelers reach Destination, resolve (or fail) Personal Goals. Players narrate outcomes. Optional: Roll 3D6 per Traveler for significant future life events (high=good, low=bad).
